7. Joseph "Matross" CLAYTON [scrapbook] 1 (Francis ) was born about 1761 in Petersburg, Chesterfield County, Virginia. He died about 1814 in New Haven, Nelson, Kentucky. He was buried in Bardstown, Kentucky.

Matross served in the military on 7 Feb 1777. He served in the military 2 in Aug 1778 in Revolutionary War US. He served in the military 3 in 1779 in Virginia. He served in the military 4 on 5 Jul 1779 in Smiths Clove. He served in the military 5 in 1780. He moved on 3 Oct 1793 to Nelson County, Kentucky from Bardstown, Kentucky. He was counted in a census 6 in 1810 in Bardstown NELSON CO, Nelson County, Kentucky.

BIOGRAPHY: Revolutionary War soldier was living in Nelson County, Kentucky by oct 3, 1793 as he was listed as a bondsman for the marriage of George Clark & Sarah Brothers a widow.

BIOGRAPHY: Joseph was thought to have come to  Bardstown Kentucky from Maryland as many Catholics settled in this area about this time.

BIOGRAPHY: Joseph was listed in the Federal Census of 1810 as living with wife and four sons and one daughter

BIOGRAPHY: MATOOSS WAS A NICKNAME GIVEN TO JOSEPH DURING THE REVOLUTIONARY WAR. HE WAS AN ASSISTANT OF AN ARTILLERY GUNNER (A MATOOSS).  by Ann Whalen

BIOGRAPHY: Virginia Revolutionary War Pension Applications:
Joseph Clayton BLWt.2474.100

BIOGRAPHY: Sept 1846, Nelson County, Kentucky William Clayton proved in Court by the oaths of Mary Clark and Susan Clark that Joseph Clayton left the following heirs: William Clayton, John Clayton, Joseph Clayton, James Clayton, Charles Clayton, Polly Ford, who was Polly Clayton who died leaving children Robert Ford, Shelton Ford,Theresa Ford, and Nelly Ford, Catharine Hayden formerly Clayton who died leaving children Elbert Hayden, Ellen Hayden, Elizabeth Hayden, Jane Hayden,
James Hayden and Urban Hayden, and Sally Clayton and Thomas Clayton, also children of Joseph, who died unmarried and without issue.

BIOGRAPHY: 22 April 1848. Nelson Co, Ky. William (X) Clayton, one of the heirs at law of Joseph Clayton, declares Joseph Clayton served during the war as a private in the Virginia Continental line.

BIOGRAPHY: 22 March 1851. Nelson Co, Ky. Peter (X) Blair, aged 84, of Washington County, Ky, declares Joseph Clayton, deceased, was a soldier in the Revolution. His brother John Blair was in service at the same time. Clayton was wounded in one of his hands. Clayton moved to Kentucky and settled in Nelson County and died in 1824 leaving as his heirs: William Clayton, John Clayton, Joseph Clayton, James Clayton, Charles Clayton, and Polly Ford who was Polly Clayton who died leaving
children Robert, Shelton, Teresa and Nelly Ford, Catherine Hayden formerly Clayton who died leaving children Robert Hayden, Ellen Hayden, Elizabeth Hayden, Jane Hayden, James, and Urban Clayton, and Sally Clayton and Thomas Clayton, also children of Joseph, who died unmarried and without issue.

Matross married Eleanor COLE, daughter of Robert COLE Jr and Anne GREENWELL. Eleanor was buried before 1848 in Nelson County, KENTUCKY.

Eleanor was counted in a census 1 in 1850 in Nelson County, KENTUCKY. She was counted in a census 2 in 1810 in Bardstown NELSON CO, Nelson County, Kentucky.
